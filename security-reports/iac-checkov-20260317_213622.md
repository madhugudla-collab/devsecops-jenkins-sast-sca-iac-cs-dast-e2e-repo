- **Risk Assessment**: Critical. Allows total control over the AWS environment.
- **Specific Fix**: Apply the principle of least privilege in IAM policies, audit regularly.
- **Compliance Impact**: Non-compliance with SOC2, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS due to inadequate access control.

#### 2. **Credentials Exposure (CKV_AWS_287)**
- **Risk Assessment**: High. Potentially exposes IAM credentials, leading to unauthorized access.
- **Specific Fix**: Review policies to prevent exposure, implement strong password policies.
- **Compliance Impact**: Violates confidentiality requirements of SOC2, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS.

#### 3. **Data Exfiltration (CKV_AWS_288)**
- **Risk Assessment**: High. Allows unauthorized data export.
- **Specific Fix**: Revise IAM policies to block unauthorized data transfers.
- **Compliance Impact**: Non-compliance with data security requirements under SOC2, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS.

#### 4. **Permissions Management (CKV_AWS_289)**
- **Risk Assessment**: High. Overly permissive policies can lead to unauthorized access and management.
- **Specific Fix**: Enforce least privilege and monitor for unauthorized access.
- **Compliance Impact**: Violates access control requirements in SOC2, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS.

#### 5. **Privilege Escalation (CKV_AWS_286)**
- **Risk Assessment**: High. Allows unauthorized users to escalate privileges.
- **Specific Fix**: Update policies to prevent escalation, enhance monitoring and auditing.
- **Compliance Impact**: Violates security principles in SOC2, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS.

#### 6. **Full Actions Permission (CKV_AWS_63 & CKV_AWS_355)**
- **Risk Assessment**: High. Grants unrestricted actions on all resources.
- **Specific Fix**: Limit actions to those necessary for specific roles, ensure compliance with least privilege principles.
- **Compliance Impact**: Violates security and resource control requirements under SOC2, HIPAA, and PCI-DSS.
